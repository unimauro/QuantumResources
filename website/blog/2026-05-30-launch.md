Quantum Resources started as a curated list of links. Today it grows into a full
**open learning hub** for quantum computing.

<!-- truncate -->

## What's new

- 🗺️ **Four learning roadmaps** — Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced and Quantum AI.
- 🧪 **Five hands-on labs** — Quantum RNG, Teleportation, Grover, Deutsch–Jozsa and a QML classifier, each with theory, runnable code and exercises.
- 🧰 **Framework guides** — Qiskit, Cirq, PennyLane, Q#, Braket and TensorFlow Quantum.
- ⚛️ **Interactive tools** — an in-browser Bloch sphere, a gate visualizer and a 2-qubit circuit builder.
- 📚 **Searchable resource library** — 500+ books plus curated courses and papers.
- 💼 **Career roadmaps** — for quantum software, research, QML and hardware roles.

## Built for everyone

Whether you are a student taking your first steps, an engineer pivoting into
quantum, or an AI practitioner curious about QML, there's now a structured path
for you. And it's all open source.

## Get involved

Found a broken link, a missing resource, or want to write a new lab? Check the
[Contribution Guide](https://github.com/unimauro/QuantumResources/blob/master/CONTRIBUTING.md)
and our good-first-issues. ⭐ a star on GitHub helps more people find the hub.

Happy computing. ⚛️
